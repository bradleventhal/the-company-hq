# Week One Post-Launch Playbook

**What to expect and do in the 7 days after launching OpenClawfice.**

---

## Overview

You just launched! 🎉

Now what?

This playbook walks you through **Days 1-7** with:
- Realistic expectations for each day
- Specific actions to take
- Red flags to watch for
- Success metrics that matter

---

## Day 1: Launch Day

### What to Expect

**First Hour:**
- Discord reactions: 5-15 🎉 emojis
- GitHub stars: 0-10
- Demo page views: 20-50
- Questions: 2-5

**First 6 Hours:**
- Discord thread: 10-30 comments
- GitHub stars: 10-30
- Demo views: 100-200
- Bug reports: 0-2 (if any)

**End of Day 1:**
- GitHub stars: 30-100 (conservative) or 100-300 (optimistic)
- Discord reactions: 50-150
- Demo views: 300-500
- First install: Within 2-6 hours

### Your Job Today

✅ **Respond to EVERY comment** (Discord, Twitter, HN, Reddit)  
✅ **Fix critical bugs** within 4 hours  
✅ **Thank early adopters** by name  
✅ **Post updates** ("5 installs in first hour!")  
✅ **Stay online** for 8-12 hours (seriously)

### Actions Checklist

- [ ] Posted to Discord #announcements
- [ ] Tweeted launch announcement
- [ ] Responded to first 5 comments
- [ ] Fixed any showstopper bugs
- [ ] Posted Show HN (if planned)
- [ ] Monitored demo page (check analytics)
- [ ] Took screenshot of GitHub stars at end of day
- [ ] Went to bed (important!)

### Red Flags

❌ **No comments after 2 hours** → Repost with different angle  
❌ **Multiple "it doesn't work" comments** → Emergency bug fix  
❌ **Negative HN votes** → Don't panic, engage constructively  
❌ **Crickets on Twitter** → Tag relevant accounts, add GIF

### Success Metrics (Day 1)

**Minimum viable launch:**
- 10+ GitHub stars
- 5+ positive reactions
- 1+ person says "this is cool"
- 0 critical bugs

**Good launch:**
- 50+ GitHub stars
- 20+ positive reactions
- 5+ people share it
- 1-2 feature requests

**Great launch:**
- 100+ GitHub stars
- 50+ positive reactions
- 10+ shares
- 5+ feature requests
- Front page of HN

## Day 3: Reality Sets In

### What to Expect

**The Dip:**
- Engagement drops another 30-40%
- Fewer comments
- Slower star growth
- Self-doubt creeps in

**This is normal.** Every launch experiences this.

**Why it happens:**
- Initial hype fades
- Only truly interested users remain
- Casual viewers moved on

**Good news:** The users who stick around are your core community.

### Your Job Today

✅ **Double down on quality** (polish what you have)  
✅ **Engage deeply** with remaining users  
✅ **Share behind-the-scenes** content  
✅ **Don't compare** to Day 1 numbers  
✅ **Focus on retention**, not growth

### Actions Checklist

- [ ] Wrote "How I built this" post
- [ ] Shared interesting use case from user
- [ ] Fixed 1-2 more bugs
- [ ] Updated docs based on questions
- [ ] Posted progress update (even if small)
- [ ] Planned Reddit/HN post for Day 4-5

### Red Flags

❌ **You stopped responding** → Community feels abandoned  
❌ **No commits in 2 days** → Looks inactive  
❌ **Obsessing over star count** → Unhealthy mindset  
❌ **Ignoring early users** → They're your evangelists!

### Success Metrics (Day 3)

**Healthy launch:**
- Still responding to comments
- 1+ commit/day
- Users helping each other
- No major bugs unfixed

**Strong launch:**
- Users posting their setups
- First community contribution

## Common Week One Mistakes

### 1. Comparing to Outliers

**Mistake:** "Product X got 1,000 stars Day 1. We only got 50."

**Reality:** Product X had:
- Existing audience
- VC backing
- Media connections
- Luck

**Your focus:** Your own growth, not theirs.

---

### 2. Ignoring Early Users

**Mistake:** Chasing new users while ignoring the 10 who installed.

**Reality:** Those 10 are your future evangelists.

**Do this:** Spend 50% of time on retention.

---

### 3. Feature Creep

**Mistake:** Trying to build every requested feature.

**Reality:** You'll burn out and ship nothing.

**Do this:** Pick 1-2 features max per week.

---

### 4. Going Radio Silent

**Mistake:** Posting Day 1, then disappearing.

**Reality:** Community assumes project is dead.

**Do this:** Post something every 2-3 days minimum.

---

### 5. Taking Criticism Personally

**Mistake:** Getting defensive when someone says "this sucks."

**Reality:** Feedback = free product advice.

**Do this:** Thank them, ask for details, improve.

## Mental Health Checklist

Launch week is **intense**. Protect yourself:

- [ ] Sleeping 7+ hours/night
- [ ] Eating real meals (not just snacks)
- [ ] Taking breaks every 2 hours
- [ ] Talking to friends/family
- [ ] Celebrating small wins
- [ ] Not refreshing GitHub stars obsessively
- [ ] Remembering: You shipped something! That's huge!

**If you're burning out:** Take 24 hours completely offline. The project will be fine.
