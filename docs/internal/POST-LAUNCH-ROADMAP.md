# Post-Launch Feature Roadmap

**What to build AFTER launch to maintain momentum and viral growth.**

---

## Week 1: Quick Wins (Viral Amplification)

### 1. Agent Leaderboard Public Page
**Why:** People love competition and showing off  
**What:** `/leaderboard` route showing top agents across all installations  
**Effort:** 2-3 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - encourages sharing

**Features:**
- Global XP leaderboard (opt-in)
- Top accomplishments this week
- "Beat this score" challenge badges
- Share your rank button

### 2. Office Templates Gallery
**Why:** Lower barrier to entry for new users  
**What:** Pre-configured office setups users can clone  
**Effort:** 3-4 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - reduces setup friction

**Templates:**
- "Solo Founder" (1 agent, max productivity)
- "Startup Squad" (3-5 agents, balanced)
- "Enterprise Team" (10+ agents, departments)
- "AI Research Lab" (specialized agents)

### 3. Animated Tutorials
**Why:** Video beats text for onboarding  
**What:** In-app tooltips + GIF walkthroughs  
**Effort:** 4-5 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - improves retention

**Tutorials:**
- "Your first 60 seconds" (auto-plays on first visit)
- "Send your first quest" (shows when clicking quest log)
- "Share your office" (shows when clicking share button)

## Week 2-4: Engagement Features

### 4. Daily Challenges
**Why:** Gives users a reason to come back  
**What:** Daily quest that changes every 24h  
**Effort:** 5-6 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - daily habit formation

**Examples:**
- "Monday Morning Standup" - Have all agents report status
- "Ship It Tuesday" - Complete 5 accomplishments
- "Collab Wednesday" - Hold a meeting
- "Focus Friday" - Single agent reaches 500 XP

**Rewards:**
- Special badges
- Unlockable office decorations
- Exclusive avatar accessories

### 5. Agent Personalities
**Why:** Makes agents feel more alive  
**What:** Unique dialogue and behaviors per agent  
**Effort:** 6-8 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - increases attachment

**Personalities:**
- **The Optimist:** Always positive, encouraging
- **The Perfectionist:** Nitpicks details, high standards
- **The Speedrunner:** Fast but sometimes sloppy
- **The Mentor:** Helps other agents, +XP bonus for team
- **The Night Owl:** More productive after 10pm

### 6. Office Customization
**Why:** People love personalizing their space  
**What:** Unlockable themes, furniture, decorations  
**Effort:** 8-10 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - increases engagement

**Options:**
- **Themes:** Dark mode, Neon cyberpunk, Cozy library, Space station
- **Furniture:** Desks, plants, whiteboards, arcade machines
- **Decorations:** Posters, trophies, RGB lights
- **Unlock via:** XP milestones, accomplishments, challenges

## Month 2: Community Features

### 7. Office Visitors
**Why:** FOMO drives engagement  
**What:** See other users' offices (read-only)  
**Effort:** 10-12 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - network effects

**Features:**
- "Visit Random Office" button
- Browse top offices (most productive, most fun, biggest team)
- Leave reactions (👍 🔥 ⚡)
- "Inspired by" attribution when copying layouts

### 8. Agent Trading Cards
**Why:** Collectibles are addictive  
**What:** Shareable cards with agent stats  
**Effort:** 6-8 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - viral sharing

**Card Design:**
- Agent avatar (pixel art)
- Stats: XP, Level, Top Skills
- Accomplishments count
- Rarity tier (Common → Legendary)
- Holographic effect for high-level agents

### 9. Office Tournaments
**Why:** Competitive events drive engagement  
**What:** Weekly competitions with real prizes  
**Effort:** 12-15 hours (includes backend)  
**Impact:** HIGH - community building

**Tournament Types:**
- "Productivity Sprint" - Most accomplishments in 48h
- "XP Race" - Highest XP gain this week
- "Team Coordination" - Best meeting participation
- "Creative Office" - Best customized office (community vote)

## Month 3: Power User Features

### 10. Office Analytics Dashboard
**Why:** Data nerds love metrics  
**What:** Detailed charts and insights  
**Effort:** 10-12 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - retention for power users

**Metrics:**
- Productivity trends (accomplishments over time)
- Agent performance comparison
- Peak hours heatmap
- Quest completion rate
- Meeting effectiveness score

### 11. Automation & Integrations
**Why:** Connect to existing workflows  
**What:** Webhooks, API integrations, Zapier  
**Effort:** 15-20 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - enterprise adoption

**Integrations:**
- **Slack:** Post accomplishments to channels
- **Discord:** Agent status updates
- **GitHub:** Auto-create quests from issues
- **Linear:** Sync tasks with office
- **Notion:** Export accomplishments

### 12. Agent Marketplace
**Why:** Let users create and share agent configs  
**What:** Community-created agent templates  
**Effort:** 20-25 hours (includes moderation)  
**Impact:** HIGH - user-generated content

**Features:**
- Upload agent configs (IDENTITY.md templates)
- Browse by category (coding, writing, research, ops)
- Rate and review agents
- One-click install
- Creator attribution

## Month 4+: Platform Features

### 13. Mobile App
**Why:** Mobile-first users want native experience  
**What:** React Native app (iOS + Android)  
**Effort:** 40-60 hours  
**Impact:** HIGH - new user segment

**Features:**
- Push notifications for accomplishments
- Quick-add quests via voice
- Swipe gestures for navigation
- Offline mode (view cached state)

### 14. Multiplayer Offices
**Why:** Collaboration is the future  
**What:** Shared offices between multiple users  
**Effort:** 30-40 hours (complex backend)  
**Impact:** VERY HIGH - viral sharing

**Features:**
- Invite collaborators to your office
- See their agents in real-time
- Shared quest log
- Team accomplishments
- Collaborative meetings

### 15. AI Coach
**Why:** Help users optimize their agent setup  
**What:** Built-in assistant that suggests improvements  
**Effort:** 25-30 hours  
**Impact:** MEDIUM - improves user success

**Suggestions:**
- "Your agents are idle 60% of the time - try these quest templates"
- "Agent X and Y work well together - assign them to meetings"
- "Your XP gain is slowing - try this daily challenge"
- "Most productive offices have 3-5 agents - consider adding more"

## Launch Week Focus

**DO NOT build new features during launch week.**

Instead:
1. Fix bugs reported by users
2. Respond to feedback
3. Monitor performance
4. Optimize based on real usage
5. Document what people actually want

**Start Week 1 features only after:**
- At least 100 active users
- No critical bugs
- Core features stable

---

## How to Prioritize Post-Launch

### Signals to Watch

**Build Leaderboard if:**
- Users sharing screenshots of XP levels
- "How do I compare to others?" questions
- Competitive behavior emerging

**Build Templates if:**
- "How do I set this up?" questions
- High drop-off after install
- Users asking for examples

**Build Daily Challenges if:**
- Usage declining after day 3
- Users checking office once then forgetting
- Need engagement boost

**Build Tutorials if:**
- Same questions repeatedly
- High confusion in Discord
- Users not discovering features

### User Feedback Driven

**Every week, ask:**
1. What did you use most?
2. What was confusing?
3. What feature do you wish existed?

**Build what's requested 3+ times before building your own ideas.**

## Anti-Roadmap (Don't Build These)

❌ **Video calls in office** - Scope creep, too complex  
❌ **Real-time collaboration editor** - Not core value prop  
❌ **Built-in AI model** - Keep dependency on OpenClaw  
❌ **Social network features** - Stay focused on productivity  
❌ **Cryptocurrency/NFTs** - Kills trust immediately  
❌ **Ad-supported tier** - Destroys user experience

## Questions?

**Q: When should we start building Week 1 features?**  
A: After launch has stabilized (2-3 days), zero critical bugs, and you've responded to initial feedback.

**Q: What if users request something not on this roadmap?**  
A: Build that instead! This roadmap is a hypothesis. Real user needs trump planned features.

**Q: Should we build everything in order?**  
A: No. Skip ahead if usage data suggests different priority. Leaderboard might not matter if users aren't competitive.

**Q: How do we know what to build next?**  
A: Look at: (1) Most requested features, (2) Biggest pain points, (3) What drives sharing/retention, (4) What competitors have that we don't.
